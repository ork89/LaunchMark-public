# **LaunchMark Privacy Policy**

**Effective date:** April 3, 2026

## 1. Overview

**LaunchMark** is a browser extension designed to help users quickly search and open their saved bookmarks through a floating search interface.

LaunchMark is built with privacy as a core principle. The extension operates **entirely locally in your browser** and does **not collect, transmit, sell, share, or remotely store any user data. All processing happens locally in the browser**.

---

## 2. What data LaunchMark accesses

To function properly, LaunchMark may access the following browser data **locally on your device only**:

- **Bookmarks** — to allow you to search and open your saved bookmarks
- **Active tab context** — only to display the search interface in the currently active page
- **Keyboard shortcut events** — only to open or close the LaunchMark launcher

This data is used **solely to provide the extension’s functionality**.

---

## 3. What LaunchMark does **not** collect

LaunchMark does **not** collect, record, remotely store, transmit, upload, sell, or share any of the following:

- your bookmarks
- your browsing history
- the contents of webpages you visit
- your search queries entered into LaunchMark
- personal information
- account information
- authentication data
- analytics or usage telemetry
- crash reports
- diagnostics
- cookies
- location data
- device identifiers

In plain terms:

## **All processing happens locally in your browser.**

## 4. No remote servers or data transmission

LaunchMark does **not** send any data to:

- external servers
- cloud services
- analytics providers
- advertisers
- third-party APIs

The extension does **not** communicate your bookmark data, search input, or usage behavior to any remote system.

---

## 5. No account or sign-in required

LaunchMark does **not** require:

- account creation
- login
- registration
- email address
- profile setup

You can use the extension without providing any personal information.

---

## 6. Data storage

LaunchMark does **not** maintain a remote database and does **not** store your data outside your browser.

The extension uses temporary in-memory processing (for example, to display bookmark search results while the extension is running), that processing remains **local to your browser session** and is not transmitted anywhere.

If future versions of LaunchMark include optional local preferences (such as UI settings or shortcut-related preferences), those settings would be stored **locally in the browser only**, unless explicitly stated otherwise.

---

## 7. Third-party services

LaunchMark does **not** use third-party analytics, advertising SDKs, tracking pixels, or data brokers.

The extension does not monetize user data.

---

## 8. Permissions explanation

LaunchMark may request browser permissions that are necessary for its functionality. These permissions are used only for the intended product behavior.

Examples include:

- **Bookmarks permission** — to read and search your saved bookmarks
- **Tab or host access** — to display the launcher on the current page

These permissions are **not used to collect or transmit data**.

---

## 9. Children’s privacy

LaunchMark does not knowingly collect any personal data from anyone, including children, because it does not collect user data at all.

---

## 10. Changes to this Privacy Policy

If LaunchMark’s privacy practices ever change, this Privacy Policy will be updated to reflect those changes clearly and transparently.

If a future version of the extension ever introduces features involving data collection, storage, synchronization, analytics, or external communication, those changes would be disclosed before or at the time they are introduced.

---

## 11. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or how LaunchMark works, you may contact the developer through the extension listing or project repository, if available.
